3. Per-Semester Analysis

Total Enrollments per Semester

Justification: This bar chart illustrates the total number of enrollments for each academic semester. It helps in identifying peak and low enrollment periods, which can be crucial for resource planning and academic scheduling.

Course Completion Rate per Semester

Justification: This visualization shows the percentage of courses completed out of total enrollments for each semester. It provides insight into student success and potential issues leading to course drops in specific semesters.

Grade Distribution per Semester

Justification: This stacked bar chart displays the count of each grade (A, B, C, D, F) awarded per semester. It allows for an assessment of overall academic performance trends and potential shifts in grading patterns over time.

4. Per-Student Profile Analysis

Distribution of GPA Among Students

Justification: This histogram visualizes the distribution of Grade Point Averages across all students. It helps understand the general academic standing of the student body and identify clusters of high, average, or low performing students.

Distribution of Total Courses Taken by Students

Justification: This bar chart shows how many students have taken a certain number of courses. It gives insight into student engagement levels and typical course loads, potentially highlighting outliers in course accumulation.

5. Summary of Data Integration Process and Insights

Data Integration Process:

Multiple Excel sheets were loaded and meticulously merged to create two comprehensive master tables: one for student-related information (`student_enrollment_course_grade_master_df`) and another for faculty-related data (`faculty_course_department_master_df`). Throughout the process, care was taken to resolve inconsistencies, such as extracting department IDs from concatenated strings and consolidating redundant columns, ensuring data cleanliness and integrity.

Insights Gained:

  • Per-Semester Trends: Visualizations of total enrollments, completion rates, and grade distributions per semester reveal clear academic cycles and can highlight semesters with notable shifts in student engagement or performance.
  • Student Academic Profiles: Analysis of GPA distribution and the number of courses taken provides a holistic view of the student body's academic standing and course load habits. These insights can be invaluable for identifying at-risk students or tailoring academic support programs.

The integrated master tables serve as a robust foundation for further in-depth analysis and decision-making in university management.
